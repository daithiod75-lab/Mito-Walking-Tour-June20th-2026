10:10 AM - 10:55 AM 45 Min Visit

Kodokan Han School

~600m (Walk: ~8-10 mins)

The largest samurai academy of the Edo period, established by Tokugawa Nariaki in 1841. Students mastered medicine, mathematics, martial arts, and Confucian philosophy.

India Connection:

Historically mirrors ancient Indian higher learning academies like Nalanda or Takshashila Gurukuls, where students resided under rigorous, holistic martial and philosophical guidance.

💡 Youth Tip:

Awesome backdrop for photos! Spot the old gate bullet holes from the 1868 Battle of Mito, showing real samurai military drama.

11:15 AM - 12:45 PM 90 Min (Lunch + Shop)

Mito Keisei Department Store

~1.5 km (Walk: ~20 mins)
🇮🇳 Cool For Indian Students:

Pass through historic old Mito Castle ruins! Look at the juxtaposition of ancient black gates against the ultra-sleek Keisei Tower architecture. Great to analyze Japanese city design.

Mito's premier modern luxury high-rise shopping hub. Perfect to enjoy air-conditioned window shopping, find top makeup, try modern cafes, and feast on the dining floor.

India Connection:

Relates directly to India's high-end lifestyle complexes like Select Citywalk in Delhi or Phoenix Palladium in Mumbai. A fun twist: the 'Depachika' (basement food hall) has the hustle and sweet scents of a boutique Indian sweet bazar!

1:05 PM - 1:55 PM 50 Min Visit

Kairakuen Garden

~1.5 km (Walk: ~20 mins)

Ranked as one of the "Three Great Gardens of Japan" (Nihon Sanmeien). Opened in 1841. Features over 3,000 plum blossom trees, deep bamboo forests, and the three-story Kobuntei building with historical tea chambers.

India Connection:

Compares to India's celebrated imperial royal landscapes, such as the Shalimar Bagh (Mughal Gardens) of Srinagar or Brindavan Gardens in Mysore, emphasizing symmetry, flowing water, and massive seasonal floral celebrations.

💡 Culture Connection:

Tokugawa Nariaki designed this specifically as a public park so everyone, from nobles to everyday citizens, could enjoy relaxation—similar to public park ethics globally!

2:10 PM - 2:35 PM 25 Min Fun

Lake Senba & Park

~1.2 km (Walk: ~15 mins)

A gorgeous calabash-shaped lake known as the oasis of Mito. Brimming with swans, ducks, and running paths.

India Connection:

Evokes the youthful energy and outdoor spirit of Sukhna Lake in Chandigarh or Kankaria Lake in Ahmedabad, where families and student groups gather for pedal boat rides, crisp lakeside walks, and street food snacks.

⚡ Fun Activities for Students & Pricing:
  • Swan Pedal Boats: ¥1,500 (30 mins, fits up to 3 people - only ¥500 each!)
  • Hand-Rowed Boats: ¥700 (30 mins, fits 2 people - only ¥350 each!)
  • Lakeside Cruiser Bicycle Rent: ¥500 per person
  • Feed Swans & Giant Koi: ¥100 per bag of food feed
2:58 PM Safely Back!

Return: Mito Station

~1.8 km (Walk: ~22 mins)

Arrive safely at the North/South entrance, completing the walking loop with perfect time left for post-3:00 PM shopping!

India Connection:

Before catching trains, don't miss trying Mito's legendary Fermented Soybeans (Natto). Texturally, it bears an incredible cooking resemblance to regional Northeast Indian delicacies like **Akhuni of Nagaland or Hawaijar of Manipur**!

Here are great dining and coffee picks located close to Keisei Department Store that cater excellently to vegetarian preferences with custom options.

100% Pure Indian Veg Available 2 min walk

Taj Mahal Mito (インド料理 タージマハル)

A beloved, authentic Indian restaurant with English speaking staff. Highly reliable vegetarian curries (Chana Masala, Dal Fry, Mix Veg) and hot buttery naans. Standard spices feel just like home.

Tip: Customize spice levels up to 5! Onion/Garlic-free options can be requested.
Premium Local Coffee Inside Keisei

Saza Coffee (サザコーヒー 水戸京成店)

The absolute legendary pride of Ibaraki's modern specialty coffee culture. Offers delicious rich matchas, oat milk replacements, and phenomenal sweet potato desserts which are naturally vegetarian!

Menu Highlights: Castella sponge cakes, Ibaraki Sweet Potato Shake.
Macrobiotic & Plant-Based 5 min walk

Organic Cafe Yosou (オーガニックカフェ ヨソウ)

Focuses on pesticide-free locally grown Ibaraki farm vegetables. They make gorgeous colorful lunch plates featuring local root crops, pesticide-free rice, and plant-derived miso soup.

Vegan-friendly, no fish dashi options available upon request.

You have a giant 3-hour window (3:00 PM to 6:00 PM) inside and directly connected to Mito Station! Here's the best strategy to maximize shopping and snacks.

Excel & Excel Minami (エクセル水戸)

Massive modern multi-story shopping complexes built directly into the station. Perfect for youth fashion, cosmetics, stationery, and traditional souvenir stores.

Native Souvenir Hunt:

Head to 3F Excel Minami's food floors! Grab standard/creative Natto (fermented soybean) snacks, plum jelly, or Mito-themed sweet bean cakes.

Fast Fashion & Pop Culture:

Check out popular stores like GU, MUJI, and Shimamura. Also excellent bookshops & fancy sticker-making purikura photo booths!

Plum / Ume Theme Specialties

Mito is famous for premium plum cultivation. Pick up non-alcoholic sweet plum juices, tangy umeboshi snack chips, and incredible plum-scented bath goods.
